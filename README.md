# AG-Writing

I became interested in food systems, agriculture, and supply chains in 2021--around the time that COVID-19 related supply chain disruptions were making headlines. The effect the disruptions had on third world countries & my neighborhood alike intrigued me, and highlighted to me just how  fragile our relationship with the earth & communities really are. I saw rich people complaining about egg prices on Nextdoor, and then read about Indonesian farmers throwing out milliions of tons of tomatoes. Why? 


In college, I studied Industrial & Systems Engineering and minored in Global Development. My major taught me the mathematical frameworks behind supply chain & manufacturing systems, and the minor introduced me to various international development concepts and paradigms. Naturally, I had an interest in the intersection of the two, and global food systems fit into that category. However, I didn't find any course or significant research at my university while I was there. 

So, I decided to do some personal research. This was in 2021, so before ChatGPT was really a thing. I learned about Zettelkasten, a German note-taking method that involved capturing ideas as Zettels, or small notes from books & articles, linking them to other notes, creating a web of interconnected ideas that writers use to formulate opinions and takes on any particular topic. 

I used this method to build up my own personal knowledge base in agriculture. I used Readwise to highlight parts of articles I found interesting, import them into Roam, and then created "Literature Notes", "Permanent Notes", which is a Zettelkasten way of saying I would summarize the highlights in my own words, annotate and draw conclusions from various articles, and then connect ideas together in this little interconnected database. 

Recently, I accepted a Venture Fellowship with a firm called the Decile Group, sponsored by a firm called Blue Impact Ventures, which invests in Latin American Supply Chain & Manufacturing startups, dabbling in Ag Tech. As such, I've been reviewing some of my old notes. I'm also in the process of moving, recreating, and revamping my ag zettelkasten from Roam Research to Obsidian. 

As such, I'm also taking a few unfinished writing projects out of the woodwork and seeing if any of them are in a state to be published.
